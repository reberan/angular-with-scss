1. Overview

The overview diagram above shows the layering from the level of the repositories to the remote services. We will move from the bottom up in the layering, i.e. from the entities in the repository to the local and finally the remote services.

The starting point are the individual entities that are labeled ENTITY in the diagram. The entities are essentially database table images with relationships represented as Java classes and annotated to take advantage of Hibernate's persistence mechanism.

The repositories in the next step then provide the basis of the persistence infrastructure and can be expanded with the necessary functionality as required.

The local services offer the externally visible functionality using the repositories.

Finally, services can also be offered in a distributed environment as remote services.
